Do's and Don'ts for Responsible Social Media Use

Do's

1\. Be real, not just impressive Instead of trying to look perfect
online, make sure your profiles (like LinkedIn or GitHub) actually show
what you can do. It's better to be honest than to exaggerate.

2\. Remember, nothing really disappears online Whatever you post can
stay there for a long time. Even small mistakes or overstatements can be
seen later by recruiters or teachers.

3\. Double-check before clicking anything If you get links, emails, or
opportunities---especially urgent ones---take a moment to verify if they
are real before responding.

4\. Think before you post or send Take a pause and ask yourself: "How
will others see this?" or "Is this safe to share?" This helps avoid
unnecessary problems.

5\. Build the habit of thinking critically Don't just accept everything
you see online. Question it, verify it, and be responsible for what you
share or believe.

Don'ts

1\. Don't post unfinished or copied work Uploading incomplete projects
or copied content just to look active can actually harm your
credibility.

2\. Don't mix casual and professional behavior The way you text friends
is not the same as how you should communicate with professors or
professionals. Keep it respectful and formal when needed.

3\. Don't trust everything just because it's easy Just because something
is quick and convenient online doesn't mean it's true or safe. Always
think twice.

4\. Don't overshare personal information Avoid sharing sensitive details
about yourself. What feels harmless now could be risky later.

5\. Don't rush your responses Replying quickly is good, but not at the
cost of clarity or professionalism. Take a moment to write properly.
